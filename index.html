The Gap

Every school teaches science.
Almost none teach making.

How does a water bottle get its shape? Why does a phone feel good in your hand? Students will answer these — not by reading, but by doing.

🎯

No Design Thinking

Students memorize answers but never learn to ask the right questions. Design starts with empathy — understanding what people need.

📦

Kit Assembly ≠ Learning

Most STEM clubs hand students pre-made kits. They follow instructions. That's Ikea furniture, not engineering.

💡

No Failure, No Growth

Real innovation requires building, failing, and iterating. Pre-designed kits are built to succeed first try. That teaches nothing.

The Program

Two levels. Four years of growth.

From creative making to professional-grade digital fabrication and smart product engineering.

Foundation

Junior Innovators

Grades 6 & 7 · 17 Sessions

  • Conduct real user interviews and empathy research
  • Rapid prototyping with paper, cardboard, clay, and tape
  • Test materials for strength, flexibility, and sustainability
  • Design product packaging with typography and color
  • Build load-bearing structures from cardboard
  • Introduction to 3D CAD modeling (TinkerCAD)
  • Wire LEDs and sensors into smart packaging
  • One-day design sprint under real constraints
Advanced

Senior Innovators

Grades 8 & 9 · 17 Sessions

  • Professional CAD in Fusion 360 (industry tool)
  • 3D printing — digital file to physical object
  • Laser cutting and precision fabrication
  • Build smart IoT products with Arduino
  • Brand identity, packaging, and ecosystem design
  • Biomimicry — engineering inspired by nature
  • Design for manufacturing (DFM) and real constraints
  • Full innovation pitch with portfolio presentation
Session Roadmap

Structured to build skills progressively.

Phase Sessions What Students Create
Foundation 1 – 5 Empathy maps, design sketches, paper prototypes, sculpted forms, material test reports
Application 6 – 10 Custom packaging, load-bearing structures, sustainable redesigns, accessible products, brand palettes
Advanced 11 – 15 Functional tools, 3D CAD models, smart interactive products, design sprint prototypes, pitch decks
Culmination 16 – 17 Polished final project, display board, live presentation at the Design Exhibition

The Gap

Students watch 1000 hours of video a year.
Almost none know how to make one.

Animation, filmmaking, and visual storytelling are the most powerful communication skills of the 21st century.

🎬

No Storytelling Education

Schools teach essay writing but never visual storytelling. A 60-second film communicates more than a 600-word essay.

📱

Every Student Has a Camera

Every phone is a film studio. Without composition, lighting, and editing skills, students just take random videos.

🎨

Creative Careers Are Booming

Animation, VFX, content creation, game design — India's media industry is worth ₹2.1 lakh crore and growing.

The Program

From flipbooks to film festivals.

Students progress from hand-drawn animation to professional 3D animation, VFX, and documentary production.

Foundation

Junior Filmmakers

Grades 6 & 7 · 17 Sessions

  • Three-act story structure, character arcs, and plot
  • Storyboarding — planning a film shot by shot
  • Hand-drawn flip book animation (12 principles)
  • Stop-motion claymation — build characters, capture clips
  • Cinematography — lighting, composition, rule of thirds
  • Video editing with free software (iMovie / Clipchamp)
  • Complete a 60-second short film from scratch
  • Anime-inspired character design workshop
Advanced

Senior Directors

Grades 8 & 9 · 17 Sessions

  • Professional screenwriting — formatted scripts with subtext
  • Digital 2D animation — walk cycles, keyframes, easing
  • 3D modeling and animation in Blender
  • Advanced cinematography — dolly, pan, depth of field
  • Sound design & Foley — soundscapes from scratch
  • Color grading in DaVinci Resolve (industry-grade, free)
  • Green screen VFX and compositing
  • Produce a 3-5 minute documentary film
